    Patchwork Farm

    We are developing a network of local growing spaces collectively known as 'the Patchwork Farm'. We run a weekly stall at the Food Market, where we sell and swap fruit, vegetables and herbs from our community growing spaces , from local gardens that we manage on a landshare basis and from local growers who have a glut of produce.


    We also supply local food producers and restaurants.

    The Foyle Pint

    Fancy drinking some free beer made by a local brewery from hops that you have grown? If so then Foyle Pint wants you!


    The palace pint it looking for more people to get involved with this great community project. Growing hops is easy! Just plant in pots or straight in the ground.


    The hops are a dwarf variety (Prima Donna), which grow to just 7-8ft so are suitable for most gardens/patios. Growers exchange tips and advice through the year on the Palace Pint Facebook page, then in the autumn the hops are collected and turned into the Palace Pint by Penge-based micro-brewery Late Knights. They donate a generous amount of beer to the growers for a 'bit of a do' once it's brewed!

    Foyle Pickup

    Foyle Pick-Ups are community clean-ups of litter hotspots that spoil our lovely neighbourhood. Some of them we then turn into community growing spaces - read how we transformed a fly tip in Galliagh into the Sensible Garden!


    The idea is to save the things people have thrown away and upcycle them into useful new objects. We've already had some plastic bag weaving workshops and want to develop further the creative aspect of waste reduction. Anyone got any ideas of a studio we can use?


    The stuff we don't save we recycle, and the bulky items we arrange for the council to collect.


    If you'd like to get involved, email annakostyrina@808.foundation

    Food Market

    Every Saturday local producers sell sustainably produced, organic meats, vegetables, fruits, salads, yoghurt, eggs, bread and fish in our market at the bottom of Haynes Lane. There are also locally made cakes and pastries and hot food - and a community stall for schools, scouts, arts groups, charities & more.


    The mix of stalls changes every week - make sure you catch your favourites and don't miss out on community activities & live performances.


    We sell meat, fish, grains, cheese, eggs, milk, veg and MUNCH more! All of the fresh produce sold at the market is organic or biodynamic, the meat is also kept to the highest standards - pasture fed where appropriate and in the best possible conditions. All our prepared food is supplied by ultra-local producers.

    Local & Fair Trade

    The Local and Fair Group encourages sustainability and fairness locally and globally by supporting the local economy and encouraging traders to stock, and people to buy, more locally produced, organic, sustainably sourced and fairly traded goods.

    Library Of Things

    Foyle Library of Things is a place where you can borrow useful Things like drills, gazebos and carpet cleaners at affordable prices and learn how to use them.


    Like many people, we often buy things that we use just once, or see things in the shop that we desperately need or want but cannot afford to buy. We want to inspire and support people in our community to spend less, waste less and share more.


    Foyle Library of Things will also create opportunities for local residents to connect and share skills with others from this richly diverse community through its events programme. These could be events like DIY classes, sessions to fix things like clothes and electronics, cooking workshops, and lots more.


    We want YOU to help us make it successful and we welcome your input and ideas - email: flot@808.foundation
